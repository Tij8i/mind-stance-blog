## **Where We Come From When We Want to Be Effective**

*This morning a reflection came to me:
 Effectiveness is not born from hyperfocus.*

Yes, concentration helps when executing tasks. But most of our work today is not pure execution — it is creativity, problem-solving, solutioning without a ready-made map. And for that, hyperfocus can narrow us too much.

We’ve all experienced it: when we let go, suddenly the insight comes. When we stop clenching, we finally grasp what was just out of reach.

The state I’m talking about is one of confidence, balance, and peace. A state where we are simply *being there*. Zen traditions describe something similar: heightened awareness without striving.

It’s not easy. Especially when deadlines loom. Staying calm and engaged at the same time — that’s the art.

For me, it comes from confidence. Confidence that I am being who I need to be, doing what I need to do. When my focus shifts from “wanting” to simply *manifesting*, progress begins.

The neuroscience even supports this. The reticular activating system filters the flood of reality into what we perceive. If I lock onto a small task, my perception narrows to that task. But if I stay open, the clues I need to reach my larger goal can still enter.

Take writing a book. If I make my first step “draft an outline,” I may realize halfway through that the outline isn’t the best starting point. Maybe I need to work on characters first. If I stay rigid, I get stuck. If I stay open, I still move toward the real goal: finishing the book.

It’s not about avoiding difficult tasks. It’s about not letting tasks become excuses for not progressing.
