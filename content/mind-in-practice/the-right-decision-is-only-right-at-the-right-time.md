*In this post, I explore how grounding, timing, and decision-making weave together. It’s about understanding that knowing something in your mind isn’t enough – life asks us to integrate, again and again.*

### **My thought today**

The right decision is only right at the right time.

I’ve been thinking about grounding lately. About what it really means to be grounded, not just in theory, but in practice. Especially when it comes to business.

I’ve noticed that I often pursue opportunities without starting from the foundations. I tell myself all sorts of stories about what’s needed to succeed – the activities, the mindset, the strategies. None of these are necessarily wrong, but for me, they are misleading when they become noise around the core.

Because the reality is simpler. It starts with doing the thing that matters most. In business, that often means creating value and bringing numbers in. Everything else builds around that.

### **A deeper reflection**

I resist this idea sometimes. Part of me resists goals altogether. I prefer thinking that life takes care of me if I just stay centered, stay myself.

But then I remember Aikido. In Aikido, it’s not enough to stand balanced and rooted. You also need to move with your partner, to blend and redirect their force into something creative. Staying balanced doesn’t mean staying still. It means being attentive, aware, attuned to what’s coming towards you, and meeting it with your own expression.

If my practice in life is about creation – about turning time into a creative force – then that is what I need to unlock each day. Creation requires space, mind space, time, freedom. And for me right now, money is part of creating that space. Business is part of creating that space.

So I come back to grounding. Grounding isn’t just a concept. It’s an action. It’s feeling your feet on the ground and moving from there.

### **Timing and decisions**

I used to think some decisions were universally right. But I see now that timing changes everything. A decision made at the wrong time, even if it’s the “right” decision in theory, can misalign everything. Like trying to blend with an attack in Aikido a split second too early or too late – you lose connection. You lose flow.

### **Seeing through illusions**

Recently I realised I’ve been deceiving myself. Measuring progress using what in the startup world are called vanity metrics, while convincing myself they were the real thing. I simplified concepts into metaphors, I structured ideas beautifully in my mind, but without grounding them in practice, they remained illusions.

Even as a kid, I remember my teacher writing in my report that I tended not to use predefined structures to build sentences. I played with words instead. It was more fun. I still believe mastery through play is different from mastery through repetition. Both are valid. But if the goal is to build a sentence, using a standard structure works.

Same with business. If the goal is to create a revenue-generating operation, sometimes the simplest way is to replicate what works. But when I sit down to work, my mind prefers to optimise, experiment, create something new, rather than just do what needs to be done.

### **Living the integration**

Accepting this about myself is one step. The next is to decide what to do with it. If I want a result, I need to work with my proclivity rather than against it. Reinvention has its place. But some moments call for repetition. Some decisions need to be made now, not tomorrow. Some lessons need to move from the mind into the body.

Because knowing something is never enough. Life asks us to integrate it again and again, each time a little deeper, until it becomes part of how we move.

### **Sources for deeper exploration**

- Tao Te Ching, Wu Wei – effortless action

- Lean Startup metrics: actionable vs vanity

- Koichi Tohei, Ki principles in daily life

### **Conclusion**

Grounding is not about standing still. Decisions are not about what is right in general. They are about what is right *now*. The question I sit with today is simple:

What is the one thing that needs to happen *today* for creation to move through me?
